It began as a WhatsApp group within a suburban residential community, just before the pandemic struck our lives in early 2020. It was formed by an enterprising young resident, a fine cook herself, with the objective of bringing GoodEarth Malhar foodies and professional and aspirant cooks on a common platform, laying out a buffet of manifold cuisine types.

It soon became a culinary lifeline with our outdoor activities practically coming to a standstill, amidst fear and social distancing. It created the much needed bonhomous bonding, bringing us closer as a community in uncertain times. It has now more than a dozen chefs and bakers who contribute regularly for commercial consumption, within and outside of Malhar. From healthy salads to consciously crafted Mille-Feuille, with continuity and innovation in styles, Tastes of Malhar is a thriving delish of a space.

Featured Home Chefs

👩‍🍳

Geeta Kamath

Malhar Patterns
Turkish Pizzas, Korean Baos & Mangalorean Curries

Geeta's cuisine is "a bit of everything", from Turkish pizzas to Korean baos, from Odisha's chhenapoda to Maharashtra's kothimbeer vadi, dal varan and zhunka, and the flavours of her hometown Mangalore, with its lovely chicken curries, fish, neer dosa, and coconut rice. As the early morning sun touches her kitchen, #paetpuja by Geeta sets out to make her friends and customers happy.

“Korean Bao with its juicy chicken filling and the softest and sweet bun is simply superb !!! My family pounced on it before I could come up with a sharing strategy.”
👩‍🍳

Bidisha Ganguly

Malhar Medley
East & West Bengal Regional Cuisine

Bidisha enjoys cooking because she enjoys eating. She grew up in Calcutta, a city known for taking its gastronomic urges to the level of decadence. She cooks from street to swanky, with a little bit of Calcutta and Bangladesh in every dish. She plans to open a very "fishy" cloud kitchen soon in Bangalore, and loves researching the etymological origins of various culinary phrases.

“Cooking is love made visible, and reading neighbor feedbacks during the pandemic was an inspiration that kept my kitchen story going.”
👩‍🍳

Sahana Kashyap Singhania

Malhar Mosaic
Indian, Mediterranean & Asian Vegetarian

Sahana grew up with a perfectionist paternal grandmother, which inspired her home chef journey. She has successfully run her cloud kitchen #homemadebysahana for several years. A self-taught cook, Sahana believes in sharing her skills freely and finds planning community menus a highly enjoyable part of her kitchen discipline.

“Authentic cooking styles combined with structural menu planning has earned rave reviews from the Malhar community every single time.”
👩‍🍳

Dr. Nafeesa Saifuddin

Malhar Patterns
Desserts, Cranberry Loaf, Brownies & Blondies

Nafeesa's foray into baking desserts began during the lockdown. Intrigued by how a handful of ingredients could transform into something so delicious, she fell in love with baking. Her cranberry loaf, brownies, and blondies are absolute favorites of Malhar, benefiting from her meticulous measuring, mixing, and creative recipe combinations.

“It did not give that warm fuzzy feeling that a birthday cake is meant to give, the kind that creates a special place in my memory account... Nafeesa's cake did.”
👩‍🍳

Sandhya

Malhar Tastes
Cakes, Breads & Emergency Baking

Sandhya began baking cakes and bread commercially after moving to Malhar in 2020. Balancing a toddler, family kitchen, and custom order baking can be a tough act, but the love she receives from neighbors strolls down the road inspires her to perfect new menu recipes. She once completed an emergency birthday cake challenge in just a few hours.

“People tell me that my preparations were a part of their family celebrations—that's the feedback that makes every day perk up.”
👩‍🍳

Rosemary Dean

Malhar Resonance
Heritage Meat Dishes & Community Platters

Rosemary channels a rich family heritage of culinary expertise into her kitchen. Her foray into food began years ago when Malhar had no delivery services, starting with community potlucks. Today, despite multiple dining options, Rose's menu remains in high demand. She often invites customers (and sometimes strangers) to dine directly with her family.

“To me, this is an element of bonding and welcoming a new family into the fold. My kitchen has opened up doors into people's hearts and souls.”

And so, Malhar’s kitchen stories continue to flourish, with more chefs and bakers joining in since its inception in October 2020. What holds this group together beyond their love of food, is the community’s support and motivation for the contributors to constantly move out of their comfort zones and experiment with new genres of cooking.

Innovation is definitely the secret ingredient in the Tastes of Malhar.
